My Journey: From Psychology to Persuasive Prose

My journey into copywriting is powered by a lifelong fascination with human behavior. As an undergraduate psychology student, I'm immersed daily in understanding the intricacies of the human mind: what motivates us, how we make decisions, and the subtle cues that influence our actions. I realized that these profound insights have incredible power when applied to how businesses communicate.

I see copywriting as more than just words on a page; it's a bridge. It's about translating a business's value into a language that speaks directly to the needs, desires, and even the unstated questions of its audience.

My Approach to Your Success:

  • A Curious Mind: Seeking to understand your business, goals, and ideal customer from a psychological perspective.
  • Strategic Thinking: Every word choice is intentional, backed by an understanding of how it will likely be perceived.
  • A Collaborative Spirit: Viewing our work as a partnership to create copy that truly reflects your brand.
  • Ethical Persuasion: Aiming to help you connect authentically, not to manipulate.

Seeing is Believing: My Initial Foray into Psychology-Driven Copy

As I build my professional portfolio, this space showcases speculative work and conceptual pieces. Each piece is an exploration of how psychological insights can be translated into effective copy.

Spec Work: Landing Page for Mindfulness App

Challenge: Increase free trial sign-ups for a new mindfulness app targeting busy young professionals.

Psychological Approach:

  • Addressed key pain points (stress, overwhelm).
  • Benefit-oriented headlines (quick relief).
  • Simulated social proof.
  • Low-friction CTA ('Free Trial').

Intended Outcome: Create immediate relevance, reduce decision fatigue, motivate sign-ups.

Conceptual Email Sequence: Fitness Challenge

Challenge: Onboard and motivate participants for a 30-day fitness challenge.

Psychological Approach:

  • Build trust and rapport (welcome email).
  • Leverage commitment & consistency principles.
  • Use goal-setting theory for motivation.
  • Incorporate scarcity for timely action.

Intended Outcome: Maximize participation, sustain engagement, foster a sense of community.

Website Homepage Rewrite: Local Bakery

Challenge: Increase online orders and foot traffic for a small local bakery.

Psychological Approach:

  • Sensory language to evoke desire.
  • Storytelling to build connection with bakers.
  • Social proof (customer quotes).
  • Clear value proposition (fresh, local, handcrafted).

Intended Outcome: Create an emotional connection, build trust, and make ordering irresistible.
